Why Watt & Co.

From the Owner

Clean panels. Labeled every breaker.

I started Watt & Co. in 2014 because I was tired of walking onto jobs where the last electrician had left exposed splices in the attic and a panel that looked like a bird's nest. That's not "old work." That's bad work.

I got my masters license at 29 after spending eight years with a commercial firm running service upgrades across the Bay. The thing I brought into Watt & Co. is simple — a panel should look like it was laid out by an engineer, not thrown together at 5:45pm on a Friday. Every wire routed cleanly. Every breaker labeled. Every junction box properly tight.

My crew is seven licensed electricians and three apprentices. No subs. No warm bodies from Craigslist. Every person who shows up at your house has been with me at least three years. That's the culture I wanted to build, and it's what I've got.

— Nate Watkins

How We Work

Four steps. Every time.

01 / INTAKE

You call, we listen

Real electrician picks up the phone — not a call center. We ask a few questions, flag if it's a live safety issue, and schedule you in.

02 / DIAGNOSE

On-site assessment

Licensed electrician shows up, diagnoses in person — panel inspection, load calc, meter check — and walks you through the fix.

03 / QUOTE

Flat-rate, written quote

You get the full price on paper before we turn a screwdriver. No mid-job surprises. No "we found something" upsells.

04 / INSTALL

Install & inspect

We pull the permit, run the install, coordinate inspection, and hand you a labeled panel with every line tested and documented.
