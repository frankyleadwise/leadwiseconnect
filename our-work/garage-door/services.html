SERVICE 01 · SPRING REPLACEMENT
Service / 01

Spring replacement.

The single most common garage door failure. Torsion springs (the long springs above the door) and extension springs (the side springs in older systems) both wear out in a finite number of cycles — typically 7-12 years for residential use, sooner for big families opening the door 8+ times a day.

We replace springs in pairs, always. If one breaks, the other is past 50% of its rated life. Replacing both saves you from a second emergency call in 6-12 months. We torque to manufacturer spec, balance the door, test the safety reverse, and stand behind the replacement with a lifetime warranty in writing.

  • Torsion & extension springs
  • Pair replacement always
  • Manufacturer spec torque
  • Door balance verified
  • Safety reverse tested
  • Lifetime warranty in writing
Response Same Day
Job Time 45-90 Min
Price Range $285-$425

SERVICE 03 · OFF-TRACK REPAIR
Service / 03

Off-track repair.

Cable snapped on one side and the door went sideways. Hit by a vehicle backing out. Came home from work to find one side of the door three feet out of the track from a windstorm. We straighten bent tracks (or replace if too compromised), pull the door back into alignment, replace cables in pairs, swap out damaged rollers, rebalance the springs.

About 80% of off-track jobs done in under two hours on the first visit. The 20% that take longer are usually because the door panels themselves got bent in the original incident — those need either panel replacement or, in worst cases, full door replacement, which we'll quote honestly on the spot.

  • Bent track straightening
  • Cable replacement (pairs)
  • Roller replacement
  • Door realignment
  • Spring rebalance
  • Vehicle damage repair

SERVICE 06 · ANNUAL TUNE-UP
Service / 06

Annual tune-up.

Florida is the worst possible climate for garage door hardware. Humidity, salt air (within 5 miles of the coast), pollen buildup, summer heat, hurricane season debris. An annual maintenance visit catches the failure that's about to happen and adds 3-5 years of service life to a properly maintained system.

What we do every visit: lubricate springs and hinges with manufacturer-approved garage door lube (not WD-40 — never WD-40), tighten every bolt, balance the door manually, test the opener safety reverse against a 2x4 (Florida code requires this annually), inspect the weatherstripping at the bottom seal, look for early signs of cable fray. About a third of our tune-up visits identify a part that's about to fail; we replace on the spot or schedule a follow-up.

  • Spring & hinge lubrication
  • Bolt torque verification
  • Door balance test
  • Safety reverse test (code)
  • Weatherstripping inspection
  • Pre-failure detection
