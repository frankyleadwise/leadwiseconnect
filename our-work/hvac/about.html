About Ironclad

Family-run since 2011. Eight NATE-certified techs.

Twenty years of fixing Tampa air conditioning. Started as a helper in 2004. Master-certified in 2011. Still on the roof when it matters.

I started as a helper in 2004 at a shop over in Brandon. Got my EPA 608 in 2007 while working full-time and going to HVAC school at night. NATE Master in 2011. That fall, I set up Ironclad in my garage in Seminole Heights with one van, a couple of gauges, and my first service agreement — a six-unit apartment building my old boss couldn't schedule.

I grew up on Tampa roofs. My dad ran commercial HVAC service for thirty years in this city before he retired. He taught me that HVAC is not about the equipment — it's about the trust. When someone calls you because their kid's bedroom is 82 degrees at midnight, you don't show up trying to sell them a platinum service plan. You fix it.

"You fix it. Then you earn the next call."

Today we're eight licensed HVAC techs, two dispatchers, and a small install crew. My wife Stacy runs the office, my brother-in-law Luis runs installs, Angela Reyes is our senior residential service tech, and Daniel Okonkwo is our emergency lead. We've done over 6,800 service calls since 2011.

We cap our service roster so we can keep same-day dispatch on most calls. We don't franchise. We don't do "memberships" that are really subscriptions. And we label every thermostat we install — with the model number, tonnage, filter size, and our direct number. Small thing. But it tells you who to call in four years when it squeaks.

— Marcus Alvarado
