About Ironclad

An HVAC company built on engineering, not commission.

We started Ironclad because Tampa deserved an HVAC company where the tech at your door is the tech who'll be back if anything goes wrong. Fifteen years later, same crew, same standard.

My dad was a diesel mechanic in Ybor City for thirty-two years. Woke up at 4:30 every morning, drove to Port Tampa, worked on freight trucks until dinner. He used to say that when something's broken, you don't patch it — you diagnose it, you fix it at the root, and you make sure it doesn't break the same way twice. That's the mechanic's code. I grew up inside it.

I started my HVAC apprenticeship when I was nineteen, right out of Hillsborough Community College. Spent my first eight years at one of the big-name Tampa HVAC chains, climbed my way up to senior tech, got my NATE master certification at twenty-seven. And the whole time I was there, something was wrong.

Techs in that company were paid on commission. Every service call had a script — "recommend the full system replacement even if the unit is fixable." Compressors that could be repaired for $400 were condemned so the company could sell $8,000 installs. Techs who didn't hit monthly upsell quotas got written up. Customers — people trying to keep their families cool in a Tampa summer — were treated like marks.

"A tech should diagnose the problem, not invent the problem."

In 2011 I left. Used my last bonus to buy a used service van, printed my NATE number on the door, and started Ironclad. The name was the promise — every job built to last, every quote iron-clad, no weasel clauses. Every tech we hire is paid hourly. Every diagnosis gets written down. Every repair gets a warranty we actually honor.

Fifteen years later we run eight NATE-certified techs, two apprentices in a state-registered program, and zero salespeople. None. My office manager handles scheduling, my techs handle diagnosis, and I personally review every system replacement quote over $6,000 before it goes out. That's the system. It's slower than the big chains, which is why they do ten times our volume and we do ten times their review score.

We'll never be the biggest HVAC company in Tampa. That was never the plan. The plan was to be the one your dad tells you to call when your AC dies in July. We're still that company. And we plan on being that company for a long time.

— Marcus Alvarado
Owner & NATE
