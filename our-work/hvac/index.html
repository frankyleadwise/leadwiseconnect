From the Owner

A family-run HVAC shop. Not a franchise.

I started Ironclad in 2011 after ten years working for one of the big chains. What pushed me out was simple — I'd spent an afternoon convincing an elderly woman she didn't need the $11,000 system my boss had quoted. She needed a $240 capacitor. I got written up for it that week.

So I built an HVAC company that pays its techs hourly instead of commission. Where every tech is NATE certified — not just one guy whose name goes on the invoice. Where we do a proper Manual J for every install, because a 3-ton system in a house that needs a 2.5-ton is the reason your AC dies young in Florida.

My wife Stacy runs the office, my brother-in-law Luis runs the install crew, and I run the service side. It's family, and it feels that way when you call — not like a call-center pitching you warranty extensions. That's the whole idea.

— Marcus Alvarado
