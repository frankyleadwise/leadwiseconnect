Coverage FAQ

Common questions about
service areas.

If your city isn't on the list, give us a call — sometimes we route to edge cities for repair work even when we don't run weekly there.

I'm right on the edge of your coverage map. Will you still come out?

For one-time work like a green-to-clean rescue or a repair call — sometimes yes, give us a call and we'll be honest about whether we can hold the response time. For weekly maintenance, we keep the radius tight on purpose. Beyond a certain drive time the route economics break and we'd rather refer you to a good local company than over-promise and under-deliver.

Do you cover commercial pools as well as residential?

We do small commercial — small condo associations under 30 units, single-location HOA pools, restaurants with patio pools. We don't pursue large-scale apartment complexes or property management portfolios. The business is built around residential and we don't want to thin the crews.

Do you charge a trip fee for outer-zone cities?

No trip charges anywhere inside the published map. The price you see is the price you pay regardless of whether you're in South Tampa or Wesley Chapel. We build routing efficiency into the pricing model, not into per-visit invoices.

What's your response time for emergency repairs?

For active members on weekly service: same-day or next-day depending on time of call. For non-member emergency repair calls: typically 24-48 hours but we triage by severity. A pump that's leaking 100 gallons an hour gets bumped to the front of the queue. A heater that won't fire mid-summer when you can swim without it goes onto the regular schedule.

Do you handle pool inspections for real estate transactions?

Yes — across both Hillsborough and Pinellas counties. Pre-purchase inspections are $285 flat with a written report turned around in 24-48 hours. We work with most of the Tampa Bay real estate brokers and title companies. Familiar with FHA, VA, and conventional lender requirements for pool reports.
