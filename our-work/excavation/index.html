Why Bedrock

Most excavation companies in Tampa are layered — a sales guy quotes the job, a project manager dispatches a crew, the crew sometimes shows up with the right machine. Costs go up, schedules slip, and nobody knows who's actually in charge.

From the Owner

IF IT'S MOVING, I'M RUNNING IT.

My grandfather ran heavy equipment in central Florida for 40 years — citrus groves, phosphate mines, the early strip malls on Dale Mabry. My dad ran a fleet for a commercial excavator in Miami. I started operating when I was seventeen, running skid steers on a residential crew in Ybor City.

I started Bedrock in 2008 with one used Cat 314 and a Ford F-550 I bought at an auction. The first year it was just me, a book of quotes, and a cell phone. I slept in the truck on long jobs. I still do sometimes.

Seventeen years later we've got twelve machines, six operators, and a dispatcher. But the core promise is the same: every job over fifteen grand, I'm on the seat of the lead machine. If you want to know how the job's going, call me — not a PM. I'll tell you straight.

— RAY CASTELLANOS
