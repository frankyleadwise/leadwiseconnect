Coverage FAQ

Common questions about service areas.

If you don't see your city above, call us — sometimes we can route to the edge cities once or twice if the job warrants it.

I am right at the edge of your coverage map. Will you still come out?

For a single inspection or one-time treatment, sometimes yes — give us a call and we'll be honest about whether we can hold the response time. For quarterly customers, we keep the map tight on purpose. The math doesn't work past the radius and we'd rather refer you to a good local company than over-promise.

Do you cover commercial as well as residential?

We do small commercial — restaurants, single-location offices, small multifamily — across the same coverage area. We don't pursue large-scale chain accounts or property management companies. The business model is built around residential, and we don't want to thin our crews over routes that don't fit.

Do you charge a trip charge for outer-zone cities?

No trip charges anywhere inside the published map. The price you see is the price you pay regardless of whether you're in South Tampa or Wesley Chapel. We build the routing efficiency into the price, not the invoice.

What is the response time during peak season?

Inspection within 4 business days is our standing commitment year-round. During peak periods (March termite swarms, July mosquito surge, October roof-rat migration), we hold to it by adding a Saturday inspection slot. Active emergencies — visible swarmers, raccoon-sized rats, bed bug bites — jump the queue and get same-day or next-day priority.

How does termite WDO work for real estate transactions?

We do Florida-state-form WDO inspections for home sales across both counties. Reports turned around in 24 hours, signed by Marco personally. We are familiar with the lender requirements for FHA, VA, and conventional buyers, and the closing-attorney protocols at all the major Tampa Bay title companies.
