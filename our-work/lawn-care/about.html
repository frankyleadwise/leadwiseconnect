About Greenwell

A landscape studio that works in seasons, not sprints.

Twenty years of quiet work across Tampa Bay's oldest gardens and its newest homes. Built on Florida natives, steady maintenance, and the conviction that a yard is part of a house — not an accessory to it.

I grew up in a family that gardened. My mom kept a small herb bed outside our kitchen in Carrollwood, my dad ran a vegetable patch in the side yard, and my grandmother — who lived two blocks away — had the kind of wild cottage garden that made the neighborhood dogs slow down when they walked past. I spent most of my childhood between those three plots with a pair of too-big pruners, getting in the way.

I went to UF on a horticulture scholarship, graduated in 2000, and spent the first decade of my career at a boutique landscape design firm in Sarasota. It was good training. The principals there cared deeply about plant palettes, about matching a garden to an architecture, about how a hedge ages over a decade. Things a lot of landscape companies — frankly, most of them — don't think about.

"A good garden is never a rushed one."

I came back to Tampa in 2005 and started Greenwell out of a friend's garage in Seminole Heights. The first year I worked alone — a truck, a trailer, one lawn mower, a hedge trimmer, and a notebook full of plant lists. My first clients were three houses on the same block in Hyde Park. I still maintain two of them.

Twenty years later we're a studio of eleven — me, three certified designers, six crew leads, and a business manager named Carol who keeps us on schedule. We take on roughly fifteen new design projects a year, and our maintenance roster is capped at 240 properties. Past that, the quality slips. So we stay small on purpose.

There's a way to do landscape that isn't mow-and-blow. It treats the yard as a living system and the client as someone who'll be there for ten years, not a one-time install. That's how we work, and that's how we'll keep working. If you want speed, there are fifty companies in Tampa who'll show up with a bigger truck. If you want a garden — we'd love to walk your property.

— Daniel Everly
