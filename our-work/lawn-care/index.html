From the Owner

A good garden is never a rushed one.

I started Greenwell in 2005 after ten years at a high-end design firm in Sarasota. The thing I learned over there, working on big estates and small urban courtyards alike, is that a landscape is a slow art. You can't force a live oak. You can't rush a boxwood hedge into maturity. Anyone who tells you they can is selling something.

So Greenwell is deliberately slow. We take on a limited number of design clients each season. Our maintenance roster has a waitlist. Every crew has a lead who's been with us at least five years, and every property we service has a site map with plant IDs, a maintenance calendar, and a photo log so the work gets better every year — not worse.

The gardens I'm proudest of are the ones nobody notices have been designed. Just a yard that looks like it belongs to the house. That's the whole goal.

— Daniel Everly
Founder &
FCLP Designer
How We Work

Four seasons. One standard.

I.

Walk the property

Onsite visit — we walk every bed, note the soil, grade, sun, existing mature plants, and listen to what you want the space to feel like.

II.

Written proposal

Plant list, materials, labor, and phasing if the budget needs it. We quote design and install separately from maintenance so you have clarity.

III.

Install on schedule

Our lead installer runs the site, permits where needed, irrigation routed before planting, and cleanup done daily — not dumped at the end.

IV.

Seasonal stewardship

We return for follow-ups, adjust the irrigation, monitor plant establishment, and roll you into a maintenance rotation if you'd like.
